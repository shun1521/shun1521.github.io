Quantum Annealing with non-stoquastic Hamiltonian

It is possible to accelerate quantum annealing by introducing quantum fluctuation other than the transverse magnetic field. An example of that is anti-ferromagnetic XX interaction. It is difficult to directly simulate this quantum fluctuation on a classical computer by quantum Monte Carlo method because a negative sign problem arises. However, by using adaptive quantum Monte Carlo method, it is known that quantum fluctuation other than transverse magnetic field can be simulated. Using this method, we are analyzing what kind of problems will enable quantum acceleration.
